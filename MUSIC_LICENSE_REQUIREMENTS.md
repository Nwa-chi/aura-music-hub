# AURA Music License Requirements

Last updated: July 2, 2026

This is the practical license packet for AURA. It is not legal advice, but it is the checklist to use before public launch, store submission, or paid traffic.

## What I can prepare vs. what the owner must obtain

I can prepare the checklist, templates, app records, license fields, source links, and release workflow.

Only the owner can actually obtain licenses because rights holders, developer programs, PROs, publishers, and licensing bodies require the legal owner/business name, address, tax/payment details, signatures, and account access.

## Minimum licenses needed for a Spotify-style app

### 1. Master recording rights

Required for each exact recording played in AURA.

Needed from:

- Record label
- Distributor
- Artist if self-released
- Beat/instrumental owner if the track is built on leased beats

Required grant:

- Interactive streaming in AURA
- Mobile/web/PWA playback
- Territory list
- Term length
- Royalty/payment terms
- Permission to store and deliver the audio from Cloudflare R2 or another CDN
- Offline/download rights if AURA later supports offline listening

Important: SoundExchange-style statutory licenses are mainly for eligible non-interactive digital radio/webcasting. AURA is an on-demand/interactive app, so commercial catalog tracks normally require direct master rights.

### 2. Composition/publishing rights

Required for the song composition: melody, lyrics, and underlying musical work.

Usually controlled by:

- Songwriters
- Music publishers
- Publishing administrators
- Collection societies

AURA will likely need:

- Public performance rights through PROs such as ASCAP, BMI, SESAC, GMR, and local/international societies depending on territories
- Mechanical rights for interactive streams and digital reproductions
- Direct publisher licenses for works not covered by blanket/collective routes

For U.S. interactive streaming, review The Mechanical Licensing Collective process for digital service providers.

### 3. Lyrics display rights

Required before displaying synchronized lyrics for copyrighted songs.

Possible sources:

- LyricFind
- Musixmatch
- Direct publisher permission
- Artist/publisher written grant for independent songs

Do not copy lyrics from lyric websites unless there is a written license.

### 4. Artwork, photos, and artist identity

Required for:

- Cover art
- Artist photos
- Profile images
- Brand logos
- Artist name, likeness, biography, and promotional copy

Use only:

- Artist-supplied assets with written permission
- Properly licensed stock/open images
- Public-domain images with proof
- AURA-owned/generated assets where commercial use is allowed

### 5. Beats, samples, loops, stems, and remixes

Required for any track containing third-party material.

Before publishing, confirm:

- Beat lease allows streaming apps, not only social media previews
- Sample clearance covers both master and composition
- Remix permission is signed by the original rights holders
- AI-generated vocals do not imitate a real artist without consent

### 6. Creative Commons and public-domain tracks

Allowed only if the license matches AURA's use.

Keep proof for each track:

- Source URL
- License URL
- Author/artist attribution
- Date checked
- Whether commercial use is allowed
- Whether derivatives/adaptation are allowed
- Whether share-alike obligations apply

Current open-catalog tracks in AURA should remain visibly attributed in the app/admin records.

### 7. User upload agreement

Before accepting uploads from users, AURA needs upload terms requiring the uploader to confirm they own or control:

- Master recording
- Composition/publishing rights
- Lyrics
- Cover art
- Samples/beats
- Artist name and likeness

Uploads should stay pending until the owner approves them.

### 8. DMCA safe-harbor setup

Because AURA allows uploads or may store user content, register a DMCA designated agent with the U.S. Copyright Office and keep agent contact information public on the AURA site.

The U.S. Copyright Office says a service provider seeking DMCA safe harbor must designate an agent, publish agent contact information on its website, and provide that information to the Copyright Office online directory.

Official links:

- U.S. Copyright Office DMCA directory: https://www.copyright.gov/dmca-directory/
- DMCA FAQ: https://www.copyright.gov/dmca-directory/faq.html

As of the checked Copyright Office FAQ, the designation fee is listed as $6 per designation, amendment, or resubmission.

### 9. Store/native rights

If AURA supports native mobile features, make sure each license covers:

- iOS app playback
- Android app playback
- Background audio
- Lock-screen controls
- Offline downloads, if added
- Push notification previews, if they mention artist/song assets
- Share links and embedded previews

## Recommended launch path

### Private beta

Use only:

- Public-domain recordings
- Creative Commons tracks that allow the intended use
- Directly signed independent artist tracks

Avoid:

- Major-label songs
- Commercial Nigerian/Turkish/Jamaican hits without signed licenses
- Unlicensed lyrics
- YouTube/TikTok/Instagram rips

### Public web launch

Before public traffic:

- Register DMCA agent
- Keep copyright/DMCA page public
- Keep upload rules public
- Verify every track has a source/license record
- Disable any track without proof
- Add attribution display for open-license content

### App Store / Play Store launch

Before store submission:

- Confirm every track is licensed for mobile app distribution
- Confirm background audio/offline rights if those features are enabled
- Prepare test account
- Prepare privacy/data safety answers
- Keep copyright contact active
- Keep support email active

## What to obtain first

1. DMCA designated agent registration.
2. Written independent artist upload/release agreement template.
3. Public performance license assessment for target territories.
4. Mechanical licensing route for U.S. interactive streaming.
5. Lyrics provider or no-lyrics policy for commercial songs.
6. Direct master/composition licenses for every non-open track.
7. Cover art and artist image permissions.
